Compound Numbers and Tens - Los Números Compuestos y las Decenas

Spanish Compound Pattern:

16-19 and 21-29 can be written as one word (dieciséis, veintiuno) or separately (diez y seis, veinte y uno). One word is more common.

Spanish Tens Rules:

20s: Written as one word (veintiuno, veintidós, etc.)
30-90s: Use "y" (and) between tens and ones (treinta y uno)
Gender: "uno" becomes "un" before masculine nouns, "una" before feminine

Large Numbers Pattern:

Millón: Masculine noun, takes plural "millones"
Billion: Spanish uses "mil millones" (thousand millions) not "millardo"
Billón: Spanish billón = English trillion (10¹²)
Important: Spanish billion system differs from English!

Key Spanish Number Rules:

1. "Y" usage: Only between tens and ones (never between hundreds and tens)
2. Gender agreement: "un/una" and hundreds endings change with noun gender
3. Cien vs. Ciento: "cien" for exactly 100, "ciento" for 101-199
4. Special hundreds: Quinientos, setecientos, novecientos (irregular forms)
5. Millón: Requires "de" when followed directly by a noun
